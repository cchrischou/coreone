It’s 2022 and we had just started to get back into the real world after covid. I started to feel like myself again. I was a senior in high school and I can still remember how it felt when I was a freshman. At the time it was so weird to even think about graduating. I even got to speak at my graduation which was a very empowering experience.

After graduating, I went to South Korea to visit my Grandma after many years. I remember feeling very nervous because in the back of my mind I knew that when I came back to the U.S. I had to go to college. The truth is I didn’t even want to go to the university I committed to. I wasn’t relieved when I graduated because my passion was in design but I settled for a major that wasn’t very strong in design as well as a university that wasn’t strong in art. What was I doing?? I couldn’t tell you. Anyway, during this trip in Korea I still had to prepare myself to go to college. Korea was still fun. I spent so much time with my Mom, Grandma, and Uncle.

Eventually I came home from Korea and spent time with friends and family. I still remember how bittersweet it was. For my 18th birthday my friends came over and I remember almost crying at their surprise cake. Afterwards, I moved into school. It was better than I expected. I met some great people and a special person. Coming home for breaks like a real college student was also weird because I remember when my older sisters used to come home during their breaks and how I always viewed that as an “old” person thing. This was truly when I started to feel like I was also getting older.
